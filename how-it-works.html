10-Minute Setup

Plug it in. Configure your rules. Lock it down. That's it.

1

Plug Into Your Router

BlockBox sits between your modem and router (or router and devices). One Ethernet cable in, one Ethernet cable out.

Works with any standard home network. No technical knowledge required.

2

Configure Your Rules

Open the BlockBox app on your phone or computer. Choose what to block:

  • Gaming platforms (Steam, Epic, Xbox Live, PlayStation Network)
  • Adult content (comprehensive filtering or custom block lists)
  • Social media (TikTok, Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, YouTube)
  • Streaming services (Netflix, Hulu, Disney+)
  • Custom domains or categories

Set time limits, schedule access windows, or block completely.

3

Set Your Commitment Lock

This is the key feature. Once configured, you choose how hard it is to undo your rules:

  • 24-Hour Delay: Changes don't take effect for 24 hours
  • Accountability Partner: Someone else must approve changes
  • Physical Access: Requires physically unplugging and resetting device (takes 5-10 minutes)

This creates friction at the exact moment you need it most—when you're tempted to disable your own rules.

4

Done. It Just Works.

BlockBox runs 24/7 in the background. All devices on your network are protected automatically.

No software to install on each device. No browser extensions to disable. No apps to uninstall.

Technical Details

How It Works Under the Hood

  • DNS Filtering: Intercepts domain name requests and blocks unwanted sites before they load
  • Deep Packet Inspection (DPI): Analyzes network traffic to identify and block specific apps and services
  • IP Blocking: Blocks known IP ranges associated with gaming servers, streaming services, etc.
  • Category-Based Filtering: Uses constantly updated databases of millions of websites organized by category
  • Local Processing: All filtering happens on-device. No data sent to cloud servers. Your browsing history stays private.
  • Encrypted Admin Panel: Configuration interface uses end-to-end encryption

Network Compatibility

  • Works with any router (TP-Link, Netgear, Linksys, Google Wifi, Eero, etc.)
  • Standard Ethernet connection (RJ45)
  • Supports networks up to 1 Gbps (does not slow down your internet)
  • Works with cable, fiber, DSL, and satellite internet
  • IPv4 and IPv6 support

Device Compatibility

  • Blocks ALL devices connected to your network (no software installation required)
  • Computers (Windows, Mac, Linux, Chromebook)
  • Phones and tablets (iPhone, Android)
  • Gaming consoles (Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo Switch)
  • Smart TVs and streaming devices (Roku, Fire TV, Apple TV)
  • IoT devices and anything else connected to your home network

Privacy & Accountability

Your Privacy is Protected

No tracking: BlockBox doesn't log specific websites you visit. It only logs blocked attempts (if you enable reporting).

Local processing: All filtering happens on the device itself. Nothing is sent to the cloud.

No data sales: We will never sell your data or browsing history. Ever.

Optional Accountability Reporting

If you want extra accountability, you can enable weekly reports sent to a trusted person (sponsor, therapist, family member).

Privacy-respecting reports: Only shows block attempt counts by category—NOT specific URLs or search terms.

Example: "12 adult content block attempts this week" (doesn't say which sites).

Limitations & Workarounds

We're honest about what BlockBox can and cannot do.

Known Workarounds

  • Mobile data: BlockBox only filters your home Wi-Fi. Phones can bypass using cellular data. (Solution: Use phone-level parental controls or accountability apps alongside BlockBox)
  • VPNs: Someone could install a VPN to bypass DNS filtering. (Solution: BlockBox can block known VPN providers, and you can enable "VPN detection" alerts)
  • Alternative DNS: Tech-savvy users can change device DNS settings to bypass filtering. (Solution: Enable "DNS enforcement mode" to block devices that don't use BlockBox's DNS)
  • Physical removal: Someone could unplug BlockBox entirely. (Solution: Use tamper-evident seals or lock the device in a secure location)
Bottom line: BlockBox is not unbreakable. A determined, tech-savvy person can work around it. But most relapses aren't planned—they're impulsive. BlockBox creates enough friction to interrupt that impulse. Used alongside personal commitment, therapy, and support groups, it's highly effective.

FAQ

Does BlockBox slow down my internet?

No. BlockBox supports up to 1 Gbps throughput and adds less than 1ms of latency. You won't notice any performance impact.

What if I need to access something temporarily?

You can configure "temporary access codes" that work for 1-4 hours. Useful for legitimate needs (e.g., researching a topic that might trigger filters). Codes must be approved by accountability partner or delayed 24 hours.

Can kids bypass this?

Not easily. BlockBox uses network-level filtering that can't be disabled by uninstalling an app. The most common bypass is using mobile data instead of home Wi-Fi—in that case, pair BlockBox with phone-level parental controls.

What if I move or change internet providers?

BlockBox is portable. Just unplug it and plug it into your new network. Your settings are saved on the device.

Is there a subscription fee?

No monthly fees. You pay once for the device. Filter database updates are free for life.
